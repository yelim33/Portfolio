Project Rationale

Is there a product that can
help families caring for dementia patients?

As technology advances, aging continues and people still fall ill. Through my grandmother who has dementia, I came to understand that age-related illnesses affect not only the patient but all family members — especially the primary caregiver. The Temi project grew from curiosity about how AI technology, without a physical presence, could be used in dementia symptom care where a caregiver's presence matters so much.

Background Information

Sundowning: The Afternoon Phenomenon
That Causes Anxiety in Dementia Patients

Sundowning refers to a condition in which dementia patients lose their ability to accurately perceive time, space, and people during the afternoon to evening hours. Patients experience anxiety and confusion, with problem behaviors such as delusions, aggression, and wandering.

Real sundowning cases found in Reddit's dementia community

DISCOVER
Literature Review

Understanding Existing Research on Sundowning through Academic Papers

We reviewed 6 academic papers on dementia sundowning, focusing on the possibilities and limitations of person-centered care models and smart home design. This confirmed the need for both emotional coping strategies and technological interventions to alleviate dementia sundowning.

⚪️Person-Centered Care (PCC)
Calm and gentle responses using 'redirection, non-confrontation, and pacification¹' are crucial for sundowning patients
Huang et al. (2023)
⚪️Potential of Home IoT
Smart homes using Home IoT sensors² help dementia patients live safely at home for longer periods
Tiersen et al. (2021)
⚪️Challenges with Technology
Elderly people's inherent resistance to technology and the risk that data may cause user anxiety exist
Lim et al. (2023)
¹ A communication technique that gently redirects the conversation without logical confrontation when a dementia patient is confused
² Utilizes cognitive, behavioral, and biometric indicators

Technical Research & Brainstorming

Designing Temi:
A Projection-Based Home IoT Hub

To answer the HMW, we researched Matter and Thread-based home IoT technologies and senior care robots. Based on this, we designed Temi — a projection-style home IoT hub placed within the patient's line of sight for emergency response — and included sensors to detect various biometric signals such as heart rate, blood pressure, body temperature, and sleep patterns.

Feasibility

MVP Core Feature Selection and Flow Design

We ideated features based on the technologies and sundowning response methods identified earlier, then organized them into 6 categories: 'AI Voice', 'Automated Response', 'Routine', 'Transitional Response', 'Environment Setting', and 'Monitoring & Alerts'.

Feature ideation

✴ Two Rounds of Impact & Effort Matrix

To determine core features, we narrowed down options through two rounds of 2×2 matrices. After analyzing the features remaining in 'Do Now', we derived the MVP of "helping caregivers respond to sundowning symptoms even when away from the patient." Finally, we organized the MVP flow in the order: notification settings → symptom detection → emergency response → result recording.

Crazy 8s

Efficiently Advancing Screens
through Collaboration

We conducted Crazy 8s with teammates to efficiently structure the screens. After sketching, we each voted for 2–3 options per screen and held about 5 minutes of discussion per screen to clarify design criteria. In this process, to focus on the core flow, we excluded onboarding and initial entry screens from scope, defined the main feature as 'emergency response', and systematized it into 'automated response' and 'direct response' based on each response type.

Final Screens

Key screens for achieving the MVP.

App Screen 1
Automated Response · Status Monitoring

Checking the Patient's Sundowning Symptoms

Caregivers can check the patient's real-time status — from the moment symptoms appeared to the current moment — through clip videos of symptom onset and hourly AI status summaries.

App Screen 2
Automated Response

Automated Response Options and Customization

Based on pre-registered patient information and what the AI has additionally learned, Temi presents optimal options to alleviate the patient's sundowning symptoms. Options include 'transitional conversation techniques' tailored to sundowning management, and an 'AI voice modeled after the caregiver's voice' for the patient's cognitive comfort.

App Screen 3
Automated Response: Additional Support

Automated Response: Personalized Additional Support

While automated response is in progress, you can check real-time patient status, information, and situational summaries. Patient-specific transitional support strategies help stabilize the patient.

App Screen 4
Direct Response

Direct Response: Communicating with the Patient

This is a response method where the caregiver communicates directly with the patient. Both real voice/video calls and text communication via AI voice are available. During the conversation, Temi provides advisory messages to help minimize conflict between the caregiver and patient.

Usability Test

Identifying AI Trust-Related Improvement Challenges

We conducted a usability test with quantitative evaluation using SUS and Task Completion, alongside interview-based Thematic Analysis. With a SUS score of 77.5 (Acceptable grade), overall usability was confirmed. However, Measured Experience revealed challenges around insufficient AI trust in core features and the need for both real-time and post-event patient status confirmation.

Key Takeaways

Trust-Centered AI UX and
Co-design Expansion Potential

We confirmed that in AI-driven products — especially senior care products — trust and peace of mind are the key decision factors, even more than operational convenience.

🔍
Need to Present AI Recommendation Rationale
To address the lack of AI trust, we need to clearly present the reasons and basis for recommendations to enhance user confidence.
💬
Strengthening Pre/Post Feedback
Strengthening the pre/post feedback system that responds to users' needs for real-time and post-event patient status confirmation is necessary.
🤝
Co-design Expansion Potential
This project was caregiver-focused, but I'd like to apply a Co-design approach where both caregivers and patients participate together for more precise design.
📊
Designing for Data Trust
From a Measured Experience perspective, the method of presenting biometric data needs to be carefully designed so it doesn't cause unnecessary anxiety in users.
